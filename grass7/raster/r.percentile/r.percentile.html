DESCRIPTION

r.tpicalculates the Topographic Position Index (TPI) of Guisan et al. (1999). The index is simply calculated by averaging a DEM over a user-specified moving window size, and subtracting the original DEM from the averaged version to get the residual. This extracts smaller scale landform information from regional scale relief. Positive TPI values represent ridges or hills, and negative TPI values represent valleys or pits.

The TPI can be used by standardizing the results and then classifying these values into generic landform components based on both topographic position and slope.

EXAMPLE

d.rast map=elev_lid792_1m@PERMANENT
g.region raster=elev_lid792_1m@PERMANENT -a
r.tpi input=elev_lid792_1m@PERMANENT size=9 output=tpi

REFERENCES

Guisan, A., S. B. Weiss, A. D. Weiss 1999. GLM versus CCA spatial modeling of plant species distribution. Plant Ecology 143: 107-122
